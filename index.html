What are wearable card payments?

Wearable payments let you tap a ring, watch, wristband or other device to pay, using tokenized card details stored securely on or behind the device.

  • • Payment rings linked to your card
  • • Smartwatches with wallet support
  • • Fitness bands with NFC
  • • Passive wearables with embedded chips

Types of payment wearables

Smartwatches

Apple Watch, Wear OS and other watches running Apple Pay / Google Pay or bank wallets.

Payment rings

Discreet NFC rings linked to your card or account, great for travel and sports.

Fitness bands

Activity trackers with NFC wallets built in for quick tap-to-pay.

Passive wearables

Bracelets, key fobs or tags with embedded payment chips, no battery required.

How wearables store card details

Layer Role
Tokenization Your real card number is replaced by a network token on the device.
Secure element Hardware or secure enclave stores the token and keys.
Wallet / app Manages cards, activation, lock/unlock and remote wipe.
NFC interface Talks to the payment terminal using EMV contactless protocols.
Issuer & network Authorize transactions just like with a physical card.

Why choose a wearable payment device?

  • • Convenience: tap with your hand, no wallet or phone needed
  • • Great for gyms, running, swimming and travel
  • • Tokenized: your real card number is never shared with the merchant
  • • Easy to lock or remove from the bank app
  • • Often works anywhere contactless cards are accepted
